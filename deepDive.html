Invitation to Future Master Thesis Students in Bio-Image Analysis

Are you passionate about bio-image analysis and eager to immerse yourself in cutting-edge research? Most of our master's theses at the University of Copenhagen are 30 ECTS, which de facto is 4 months of research time, and this often feels too short. Often, it is at the point of hand-in that we are ready to answer the truly exciting and challenging questions.

However, if you are a motivated student who seeks a richer experience, you can extend your research time by combining it with projects. In the Master of Computer Science education and other programs, it is possible to do a 15 ECTS project outside the study curriculum and a 7.5 ECTS master thesis preparation project to make your final year a deeper dive. If you choose so, we will top this up by helping you design a coherent set of projects, access to lab and office facilities at the image section, be part of our research community, and thus become a junior member of our section under either Julius' or Jon's supervision.

You will:

  • Work closely with us and our research group for up to 12 months
  • Access our facilities and tools for bio-image analysis
  • Be part of a vibrant, collaborative research environment
  • Build skills and results that go far beyond the standard thesis timeframe

If you are ready to explore, innovate, and truly experience research from the inside, we will be happy to hear from you. The seating will be limited.

Julius Kirkegaard and Jon Sporring Data Science Lab, Image Section, Department of Computer Science, University of Copenhagen
